CC   -!- FUNCTION: Plays an important role in protecting the acceptor side
CC       of photosystem II (PSII) against oxidative damage, especially
CC       under iron-limiting growth conditions (Probable). The differing
CC       subcellular locations of futA1 (predominantly thylakoid lumen) and
CC       futA2 (predominantly periplasmic) suggest they may fulfill
CC       different roles. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11292796, ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: A major iron-binding protein involved in Fe(3+) uptake,
CC       probably part of a periplasmic ABC transporter complex futA1A2BC
CC       (TC 3.A.1.10.2) involved in Fe(3+) ion import (ferric iron). This
CC       protein and futA2 (slr0531) may be subunit proteins that have
CC       redundant or overlapping substrate-binding functions (Probable).
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:11292796}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cellular thylakoid membrane {ECO:0000305};
CC       Lipid-anchor {ECO:0000305}. Cell membrane {ECO:0000305}; Lipid-
CC       anchor {ECO:0000305}. Note=Localization to the periplasm was not
CC       detected in PubMed:12368463. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12368463,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16287171, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16400685}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Transcript levels increase when cells are grown in the
CC       absence of iron. Protein levels increase when cells are grown in
CC       the absence of iron. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11292796,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12368463}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Cells grow normally in the absence of iron
CC       but have a reduced capacity for Fe(3+) uptake; double knockouts of
CC       this gene and futA2 (slr0531) grow very poorly in the absence of
CC       iron and have a marked reduction in their ability to take up
CC       Fe(3+). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11292796,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12368463}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the bacterial solute-binding protein 1
CC       family. {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- CAUTION: There is controversy as to whether this protein
CC       preferentially binds Fe(2+) or Fe(3+), and also whether it is
CC       found associated with the plasma membrane or not. {ECO:0000305}.
