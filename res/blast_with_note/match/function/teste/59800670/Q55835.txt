CC   -!- FUNCTION: Probably part of a periplasmic ABC transporter complex
CC       futA1A2BC (TC 3.A.1.10.2) involved in Fe(3+) ion import (ferric
CC       iron). This protein and futA1 (slr1295) are subunit proteins that
CC       have redundant or overlapping substrate-binding functions
CC       (Probable). The differing subcellular locations of futA1
CC       (predominantly thylakoid lumen) and futA2 (predominantly
CC       periplasmic) suggest they may fulfill different roles.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: Plays an important role in protecting the acceptor side
CC       of photosystem II (PSII) against oxidative damage, especially
CC       under iron-limiting growth conditions. {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: Plays an undefined role in copper supply to thylakoid
CC       proteins.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cellular thylakoid membrane {ECO:0000305};
CC       Peripheral membrane protein {ECO:0000305}; Lumenal side
CC       {ECO:0000305}. Periplasm. Note=Binds Fe(3+) in the periplasm.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Transcript levels increase when cells are grown in the
CC       absence of iron. Periplasmic levels increase when cells are grown
CC       in high NaCl or in the absence of iron.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10998049, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11292796,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12368463}.
CC   -!- PTM: Predicted to be exported by the Tat system. The position of
CC       the signal peptide cleavage has not been experimentally proven.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Cells grow normally in the absence of iron;
CC       double knockouts of this gene and futA1 (slr1295) grow very poorly
CC       in the absence of iron and have a marked reduction in their
CC       ability to take up Fe(3+). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11292796}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the bacterial solute-binding protein 1
CC       family. {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- CAUTION: There is some controversy as to whether this protein
CC       preferentially binds Fe(2+) or Fe(3+). {ECO:0000305}.
