CC   -!- FUNCTION: Functions as enzyme, and as scaffolding protein that
CC       modulates transcriptional activation by the LEF1/TCF1-CTNNB1
CC       complex and by the complex formed with MITF and CTNNB1. Modulates
CC       p53/TP53 levels and p53/TP53-mediated apoptosis. Modulates
CC       proteasomal degradation of target proteins by the SCF (SKP2-CUL1-
CC       F-box protein) E3 ubiquitin-protein ligase complex (By
CC       similarity). Hydrolyzes purine nucleotide phosphoramidates,
CC       including adenosine 5'monophosphoramidate (AMP-NH2), adenosine
CC       5'monophosphomorpholidate (AMP-morpholidate) and guanosine
CC       5'monophosphomorpholidate (GMP-morpholidate). Hydrolyzes lysyl-AMP
CC       (AMP-N-epsilon-(N-alpha-acetyl lysine methyl ester)) generated by
CC       lysine tRNA ligase, lysyl-GMP (GMP-N-epsilon-(N-alpha-acetyl
CC       lysine methyl ester)) and AMP-N-alanine methyl ester. Can also
CC       convert adenosine 5'-O-phosphorothioate and guanosine 5'-O-
CC       phosphorothioate to the corresponding nucleoside 5'-O-phosphates
CC       with concomitant release of hydrogen sulfide. {ECO:0000250,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11805111, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15703176,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20940308}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. Interacts with CDK7. Interacts with RUVBL1 and
CC       RUVBL2 and is associated with the LEF1/TCF1-CTNNB1 complex and
CC       with a KAT5 histone acetyltransferase complex. Identified in a
CC       complex with MITF and CTNNB1. Interacts with CDC34 and RBX1, and
CC       is part of a SCF (SKP2-CUL1-F-box protein) E3 ubiquitin-protein
CC       ligase complex (By similarity). {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm. Nucleus {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Widely expressed.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the HINT family. {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Contains 1 HIT domain. {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-
CC       ProRule:PRU00464}.
CC   -!- CAUTION: Was originally thought to be a protein kinase C inhibitor
CC       and to bind zinc in solution. Both seem to be incorrect.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
