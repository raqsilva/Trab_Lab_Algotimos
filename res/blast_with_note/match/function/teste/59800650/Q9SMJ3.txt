CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the conversion of zeta-carotene to lycopene
CC       via the intermediary of neurosporene. It carries out two
CC       consecutive desaturations (introduction of double bonds) at
CC       positions C-7 and C-7'. Shows stereoselectivity toward trans C15-
CC       C15'zeta-carotene double bond. The zeta-carotene produced by the
CC       phytoene desaturase PDS has a C15-C15' double bond in the cis
CC       configuration and it requires isomerization before being
CC       recognized as substrate by ZDS. No activity with all-trans-zeta-
CC       carotene. The main product is 7,9,7',9'-tetra-cis-lycopene (pro-
CC       lycopene). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10491195,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15503129}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: 9,9'-di-cis-zeta-carotene + 2 quinone =
CC       7,9,7',9'-tetra-cis-lycopene + 2 quinol.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10491195, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15503129}.
