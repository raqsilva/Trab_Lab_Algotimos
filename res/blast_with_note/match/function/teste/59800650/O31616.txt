CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the FAD-dependent oxidative deamination of
CC       various amines and D-amino acids to yield the corresponding alpha-
CC       keto acids, ammonia/amine, and hydrogen peroxide. Oxidizes
CC       sarcosine (N-methylglycine), N-ethylglycine and glycine. Can also
CC       oxidize the herbicide glyphosate (N-phosphonomethylglycine).
CC       Displays lower activities on D-alanine, D-valine, D-proline and D-
CC       methionine. Does not act on L-amino acids and other D-amino acids.
CC       Is essential for thiamine biosynthesis since the oxidation of
CC       glycine catalyzed by ThiO generates the glycine imine intermediate
CC       (dehydroglycine) required for the biosynthesis of the thiazole
CC       ring of thiamine pyrophosphate. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12627963,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9827558}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Glycine + H(2)O + O(2) = glyoxylate + NH(3) +
CC       H(2)O(2). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19864430,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9827558}.
