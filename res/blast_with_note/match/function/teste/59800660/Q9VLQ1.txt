CC   -!- FUNCTION: Required for normal production of N-glycosylated
CC       proteins in the endoplasmic reticulum (ER). Required for embryonic
CC       segmentation, dorsal-ventral patterning and gastrulation. Required
CC       for chitin orientation and shaping of the apical and lateral
CC       plasma membranes of epidermal cells during cuticle
CC       differentiation. Also required for correctly shaping apical
CC       membrane topology of the epithelia of other organs such as the
CC       midgut and the hindgut. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18403407,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21199819}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: UDP-glucose + dolichyl phosphate = UDP +
CC       dolichyl beta-D-glucosyl phosphate.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y673}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Protein modification; protein glycosylation.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9Y673}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Endoplasmic reticulum membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21199819}; Single-pass type II membrane
CC       protein {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21199819}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Expressed maternally and zygotically. In
CC       cellularizing embryos, maternal expression is ubiquitous. At
CC       embryonic stage 11, zygotic expression begins in salivary gland
CC       precursor cells. In stage 16 embryos, strong expression in
CC       salivary glands and part of the proventriculus is detected.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18403407}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Larval lethality. Embryos lacking maternal
CC       and zygotic wol activity show segmentation, dorso-ventral
CC       patterning and gastrulation defects. As a result, germband
CC       elongation does not proceed normally and mesoderm invagination is
CC       disturbed. In these embryos, protein glycosylation is reduced by
CC       25 percent, the ER tubules are smooth and the unfolded protein
CC       response (UPR), a transcriptional response which up-regulates
CC       genes that enable cells to cope with misfolded, endoplasmic
CC       reticulum-retained proteins, is activated. Larvae lacking maternal
CC       wol present patterning and morphological defects, including the
CC       failure to form a normal head skeleton, a discontinuous cuticle
CC       and irregular body contours. Larvae lacking both maternal and
CC       zygotic wol show severe reduction in cuticle deposition, a
CC       complete failure of denticle formation and melanization, loss of
CC       chitin orientation in the procuticle and dislocation of proteins
CC       of the lower electron-dense portion of the epicuticle into the
CC       upper electron-lucid sublayer. In addition, the midgut microvilli
CC       are separated from each other by large gaps and the apical plasma
CC       membrane of the hindgut is highly irregular.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18403407, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21199819}.
