CC   -!- FUNCTION: DNA polymerase III is a complex, multichain enzyme
CC       responsible for most of the replicative synthesis in bacteria.
CC       This DNA polymerase also exhibits 3' to 5' exonuclease activity.
CC       The beta chain is required for initiation of replication; once it
CC       is clamped onto DNA it slides freely (bidirectional and ATP-
CC       independent) along duplex DNA. Coordinates protein traffic at the
CC       replication fork, where it interacts with multiple DNA
CC       polymerases. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15466025,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22716942}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Deoxynucleoside triphosphate + DNA(n) =
CC       diphosphate + DNA(n+1).
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: The DNA polymerase holoenzyme is a complex that contains
CC       10 different types of subunits. These subunits are organized into
CC       3 functionally essential subassemblies: the pol III core, the beta
CC       sliding clamp processivity factor and the clamp-loading complex.
CC       The pol III core (subunits alpha, epsilon and theta) contains the
CC       polymerase and the 3'-5' exonuclease proofreading activities. The
CC       polymerase is tethered to the template via the beta sliding clamp
CC       processivity factor (this entry). The clamp-loading complex
CC       assembles the beta processivity factor onto the primer template
CC       and plays a central role in the organization and communication at
CC       the replication fork. This complex contains delta, delta', psi and
CC       chi, and copies of either or both of two different DnaX proteins,
CC       gamma and tau. The composition of the holoenzyme is, therefore:
CC       (alpha,epsilon,theta)[2]-(gamma/tau)[3]-delta,delta', psi,chi-
CC       beta[4]. The beta chain is a homodimer when not associated with
CC       the other components. Can also be part of the RIDA complex
CC       (regulatory inactivation of DnaA), consisting of ATP-DnaA, ADP-Hda
CC       and DNA-loaded beta clamp. Probably also interacts with components
CC       of DNA polymerases I, II, IV and V. Binds to CrfC homooligomers at
CC       the midcell position during DNA replication.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14592985, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15150238,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15466025, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18977760,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23994470}.

CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: The temperature- and UV-sensitive allele dnaN159
CC       does not grow at higher than 37 degrees Celsius. The global SOS
CC       response is chronically induced. The UV-sensitivity of dnaN159 is

CC       mutation rate (umuC, umuD), and is absolutely dependent on the
CC       polymerase activity of Pol I (polA) for viability.
