CC   -!- FUNCTION: Galactosyl transferase is essential for the assembly of
CC       the group B streptococci (GBS) type III capsular polysaccharide.
CC       May be involved in the formation of either or both galactosidic
CC       bonds by catalyzing the addition of galactose to an
CC       oligosaccharide precursor or to a lipid intermediate. Type III
CC       capsular polysaccharide consists of a linear backbone with short
CC       side chains ending in residues of N-acetylneuraminic acid or
CC       sialic acid. The presence of sialic acid on the surface of the
CC       organism inhibits activation of the alternative pathway of
CC       complement and is thought to be an important element in the
CC       virulence function of the capsule (By similarity). {ECO:0000250}.
