CC   -!- FUNCTION: Couples transcription and DNA repair by recognizing RNA
CC       polymerase (RNAP) stalled at DNA lesions. Mediates ATP-dependent
CC       release of RNAP and its truncated transcript from the DNA, and
CC       recruitment of nucleotide excision repair machinery to the damaged
CC       site. Can also dissociate RNAP that is blocked by low
CC       concentration of nucleoside triphosphates or by physical
CC       obstruction, such as bound proteins. In addition, can rescue
CC       arrested complexes by promoting forward translocation. Has ATPase
CC       activity, which is required for removal of stalled RNAP, but seems
CC       to lack helicase activity. May act through a translocase activity
CC       that rewinds upstream DNA, leading either to translocation or to
CC       release of RNAP when the enzyme active site can not continue
CC       elongation. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_00969,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12086674, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19700770,
