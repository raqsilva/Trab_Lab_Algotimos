CC   -!- FUNCTION: Trifunctional enzyme involved in UDP-beta-L-rhamnose
CC       biosynthesis, a precursor of the primary cell wall components
CC       rhamnogalacturonan I (RG-I) and rhamnogalacturonan II (RG-II)
CC       (PubMed:17190829). Catalyzes the dehydration of UDP-glucose to
CC       form UDP-4-dehydro-6-deoxy-D-glucose followed by the epimerization
CC       of the C3' and C5' positions of UDP-4-dehydro-6-deoxy-D-glucose to
CC       form UDP-4-keto-beta-L-rhamnose and the reduction of UDP-4-keto-
CC       beta-L-rhamnose to yield UDP-beta-L-rhamnose (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9LPG6, ECO:0000269|PubMed:14671019,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17190829}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: UDP-glucose = UDP-4-dehydro-6-deoxy-D-glucose
CC       + H(2)O. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9LPG6}.
CC   -!- COFACTOR:
CC       Name=NAD(+); Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:57540;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9LPG6};
CC   -!- COFACTOR:
CC       Name=NADP(+); Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:58349;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9LPG6};
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Carbohydrate biosynthesis. {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in roots, stems, seedlings, and
CC       siliques. Lower expression in inflorescence tips, and leaves.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14671019, ECO:0000269|PubMed:14701918}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The dehydratase activity is contained in the N-terminal
CC       region while the epimerase and reductase activities are in the C-
CC       terminal region. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9LPG6}.
CC   -!- PTM: Ubiquitinated. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19292762}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: In bacteria, TDP-L-rhamnose is formed by the
CC       successive action of three different enzymes on TDP-D-glucose. In
CC       plants, on the other hand, a single polypeptide probably catalyzes
CC       all three reactions that lead to the conversion of UDP-D-glucose
CC       to UDP-L-rhamnose.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: In the N-terminal section; belongs to the NAD(P)-
CC       dependent epimerase/dehydratase family. dTDP-glucose dehydratase
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: In the C-terminal section; belongs to the dTDP-4-
CC       dehydrorhamnose reductase family. {ECO:0000305}.
