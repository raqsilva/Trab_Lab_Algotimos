CC   -!- FUNCTION: Trifunctional enzyme involved in UDP-beta-L-rhamnose
CC       biosynthesis, a precursor of the primary cell wall components
CC       rhamnogalacturonan I (RG-I) and rhamnogalacturonan II (RG-II).
CC       Catalyzes the dehydration of UDP-glucose to form UDP-4-dehydro-6-
CC       deoxy-D-glucose followed by the epimerization of the C3' and C5'
CC       positions of UDP-4-dehydro-6-deoxy-D-glucose to form UDP-4-keto-
CC       beta-L-rhamnose and the reduction of UDP-4-keto-beta-L-rhamnose to
CC       yield UDP-beta-L-rhamnose (PubMed:17190829). Required for the
CC       normal seed coat epidermal development (PubMed:14671019).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14671019, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17190829}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: UDP-glucose = UDP-4-dehydro-6-deoxy-D-glucose
CC       + H(2)O. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17190829}.
CC   -!- COFACTOR:

CC   -!- PATHWAY: Carbohydrate biosynthesis. {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in roots, stems, leaves, seedlings,
CC       inflorescence tips, and siliques. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14671019}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Up-regulated at the time of mucilage
CC       production during seed coat differentiation.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14701918}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The dehydratase activity is contained in the N-terminal
CC       region while the epimerase and reductase activities are in the C-
CC       terminal region. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17190829}.
CC   -!- PTM: Ubiquitinated. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19292762}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: In bacteria, TDP-L-rhamnose is formed by the
CC       successive action of three different enzymes on TDP-D-glucose. In
CC       plants, on the other hand, a single polypeptide probably catalyzes
CC       all three reactions that lead to the conversion of UDP-D-glucose
CC       to UDP-L-rhamnose.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: RHM2 appears to be more highly expressed in cv.
CC       Landsberg erecta than in cv. Col-2. Transcriptions factors AP2,
CC       TTG1, and GL2 are required for maximum levels of RHM2 expression
CC       at the time of mucilage production.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: In the N-terminal section; belongs to the NAD(P)-
CC       dependent epimerase/dehydratase family. dTDP-glucose dehydratase
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: In the C-terminal section; belongs to the dTDP-4-
CC       dehydrorhamnose reductase family. {ECO:0000305}.
