CC   -!- FUNCTION: Facilitates transcription termination by a mechanism
CC       that involves Rho binding to the nascent RNA, activation of Rho's
CC       RNA-dependent ATPase activity, and release of the mRNA from the
CC       DNA template. Shows poor RNA-dependent ATP hydrolysis and
CC       inefficient DNA-RNA unwinding activities, but exhibits robust and
CC       fast transcription termination, which suggests that the
CC       transcription termination function of M.tuberculosis Rho is not
CC       correlated with its helicase/translocase activities and that these
CC       functions may not be important for its RNA release process.
CC       {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_01884, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20026069}.
