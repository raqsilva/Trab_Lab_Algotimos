CC   -!- FUNCTION: May be directly involved in the regulation of the rib
CC       genes. C-terminal part of RibR specifically binds to RFN of the
CC       rib leader of the riboflavin biosynthetic operon. The RFN element
CC       is a sequence within the rib-leader mRNA reported to serve as a
CC       receptor for an FMN-dependent riboswitch. Possibly, RibR produces
CC       the comodulator FMN through its own N-terminal flavokinase
CC       activity. FMN-activated RibR may stabilize the anti-anti
CC       terminator structure of RFN mRNA, causing transcription
CC       termination of the rib genes in trans.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15668000, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17590224}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + riboflavin = ADP + FMN.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Repressed by the HTH-type transcriptional regulator
CC       CymR. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17590224}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Mutants lacking this gene are deregulated
CC       with respect to flavin (riboflavin, FMN and FAD) biosynthesis.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15668000}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Overexpression of RibR suppresses the riboflavin-
CC       overproducing ribC-defective phenotype, which is the major
CC       riboflavin kinase responsible of FMN biosynthesis by riboflavin
CC       phosphorylation (PubMed:17590224 and PubMed:15668000).
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the RibR family. {ECO:0000305}.
