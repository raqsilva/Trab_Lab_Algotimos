CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the phosphorylation of riboflavin (vitamin B2)
CC       to form flavin-mononucleotide (FMN), hence rate-limiting enzyme in
CC       the synthesis of FAD. Essential for TNF-induced reactive oxygen
CC       species (ROS) production. Through its interaction with both
CC       TNFRSF1A and CYBA, physically and functionally couples TNFRSF1A to
CC       NADPH oxidase. TNF-activation of RFK may enhance the incorporation
CC       of FAD in NADPH oxidase, a critical step for the assembly and
CC       activation of NADPH oxidase (By similarity). {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + riboflavin = ADP + FMN.

CC       Note=Zinc or magnesium. {ECO:0000250};
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Cofactor biosynthesis; FMN biosynthesis; FMN from
CC       riboflavin (ATP route): step 1/1.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Monomer (By similarity). Directly interacts with TNFRSF1A
CC       death domain; this interaction may be supported by TRADD. In the
CC       absence of TNFRSF1A, interacts with TRADD. Independently of
CC       TNFRSF1A, interacts with the NADPH oxidase subunit CYBA.
