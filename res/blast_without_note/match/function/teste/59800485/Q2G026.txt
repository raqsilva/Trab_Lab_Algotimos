CC   -!- FUNCTION: Subunit of the protein translocation channel SecYEG (By
CC       similarity). While not essential, it considerably increases the
CC       export efficiency of extracellular proteins. {ECO:0000250,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20472795}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the Sec protein translocase complex.
CC       Heterotrimer consisting of SecY, SecE and SecG subunits. The
CC       heterotrimers can form oligomers, although 1 heterotrimer is
CC       thought to be able to translocate proteins. Interacts with SecDF,
CC       and other proteins may be involved. The channel interacts with
CC       SecA via subunit SecY (By similarity). Also part of the accessory
CC       SecA2/SecY2 protein translocation apparatus required to export
CC       cell wall protein GspB. {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000250}; Multi-pass
CC       membrane protein {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Grows normally, a double secG/secY2 mutant
CC       enters stationary phase earlier. Significant differences in the
CC       export pattern of a number of extracellular proteins are seen in a
CC       secG deletion; the double secG/secY2 mutant exacerbates this
CC       effect (shown in RN4220). Single or double deletions are as
CC       virulent as wild-type (shown in RN4220 and SH1000).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20472795}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the SecG family. {ECO:0000305}.
