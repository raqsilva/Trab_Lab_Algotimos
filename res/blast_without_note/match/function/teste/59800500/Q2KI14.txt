CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalytic subunit of the N-terminal acetyltransferase A
CC       (NatC) complex which displays alpha (N-terminal) acetyltransferase
CC       activity. Without NAA15, displays epsilon (internal)
CC       acetyltransferase activity towards HIF1A, thereby promoting its
CC       degradation. Represses MYLK kinase activity by acetylation, and
CC       thus represses tumor cell migration. Acetylates, and stabilizes
CC       TSC2, thereby repressing mTOR activity and suppressing cancer
CC       development.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Acetyl-CoA + peptide = N(alpha)-acetylpeptide
CC       + CoA.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the N-terminal acetyltransferase A (NatA)
CC       complex composed of NAA10 and NAA15 or NAA16. Interacts with the
CC       ribosome. Interacts with HIF1A and NAA50. Binds to MYLK.
CC       Associates with HYPK when in complex with NAA15. Interacts (via
CC       its C-terminal domain) with TSC2, leading to its acetylation (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250}. Nucleus
CC       {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- PTM: Cleaved by caspases during apoptosis. {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylation by IKBKB/IKKB at Ser-209 destabilises NAA10
CC       and promotes its proteasome-mediated degradation. {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the acetyltransferase family. ARD1
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
