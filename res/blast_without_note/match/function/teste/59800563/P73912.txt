CC   -!- FUNCTION: Required during biogenesis of c-type cytochromes
CC       (cytochrome c6 and cytochrome f) at the step of heme attachment.
CC       {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: Probably not involved in heme transport or reduction as
CC       mutants deleted for the first 24 residues cannot be rescued by
CC       growth in the presence of heme or reductant.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10542282}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: May interact with CcsA. {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cellular thylakoid membrane {ECO:0000250};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Bacteria lacking the N-terminus of CcsB are able to
CC       grow anaerobically but not aerobically, suggesting its function is
CC       essential for aerobic growth. In these bacteria reduced amounts of
CC       some holo-c-type cytochromes are observed (cytochrome f and
CC       cytochrome c550); unprocessed precursor apocytochrome f
CC       accumulates but is not thought to be integrated into the
CC       cytochrome b6-f complex. Holo-cytochrome c553 (also known as
CC       cytochrome c6) does not accumulate however.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the Ccs1/CcsB family. {ECO:0000305}.
