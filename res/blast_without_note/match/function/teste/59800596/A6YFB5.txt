CC   -!- FUNCTION: Serine protease with a variety of targets, including
CC       extracellular matrix proteins and proteoglycans such as biglycan,
CC       syndecan-4 and glypican-4. Through cleavage of proteoglycans, may
CC       release soluble FGF-glycosaminoglycan complexes that promote the
CC       range and intensity of FGF signals in the extracellular space.
CC       Consequently, facilitates inductive processes in the developping
CC       embryo, such as posteriorization, mesoderm induction and neuronal
CC       differentiation. Regulates the availability of insulin-like growth
CC       factors (IGFs) by cleaving IGF-binding proteins. Inhibits
CC       signaling mediated by TGF-beta family members. Consequently, may
CC       regulate many physiological processes. Intracellularly, degrades
CC       TSC2, leading to the activation of TSC2 downstream targets.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17681134}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Forms homotrimers. In the presence of substrate, may form
CC       higher-order multimers in a PDZ-independent manner. {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Secreted {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17681134}.
CC       Cytoplasm, cytosol {ECO:0000250}. Note=Also found associated with
CC       the plasma membrane. {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: First detected after midblastula transition.
CC       Expression increases during gastrulation and neurulation.
CC       Expressed in the blastopore lip at gastrula stage (stage 11), the
CC       posterior mesoderm and anterior neural plate after involution
CC       (stage 14), the early forebrain and midbrain-hindbrain boundary at
CC       the neurula stage (stage 16), and the branchial arch region in
CC       tail bud stage embryos (stage 26). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17681134}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Up-regulated by FGF4 and FGF8.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17681134}.
