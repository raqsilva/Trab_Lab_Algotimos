CC   -!- FUNCTION: Required for the formation of a threonylcarbamoyl group
CC       on adenosine at position 37 (t(6)A37) in tRNAs that read codons
CC       beginning with adenine. Is probably involved in the transfer of
CC       the threonylcarbamoyl moiety of threonylcarbamoyl-AMP (TC-AMP) to
CC       the N6 group of A37, together with TsaE and TsaB. TsaD likely
CC       plays a direct catalytic role in this reaction. May also be
CC       involved in the metabolism of glycated proteins, but does not show
CC       sialoglycoprotease activity against glycophorin A.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20824107, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21183954,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22378793}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: L-threonylcarbamoyladenylate + adenine(37) in
CC       tRNA = AMP + N(6)-L-threonylcarbamoyladenine(37) in tRNA.
CC       {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_01445, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22378793}.
CC   -!- COFACTOR:

CC       Note=Binds 1 Fe(2+) ion per subunit. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-
CC       Rule:MF_01445};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. Interacts with TsaB; may form a heterodimer
CC       with TsaB. Also interacts with TsaC. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19376873,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20701780, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21285948,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22378793}.

CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19376873}.
CC   -!- PTM: Can be proteolytically processed in vitro by TsaB.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19376873}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Appears essential for growth, since no null
CC       mutants can be obtained. Conditional depletion of this gene
CC       prevents t(6)A37 modification, and leads to pleiotropic phenotypes
CC       including increased or reduced cell size, unusual distribution of
CC       DNA around the cell periphery, nucleoid loss, and membrane/cell
CC       envelope defects. These phenotypes could be indirect effects of
CC       severe translation defects. The TsaD depletion phenotype is
CC       suppressed by overexpressing the response regulator RstA.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19376873, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19578062,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21183954, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9743119}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: TsaBCDE are necessary and sufficient for tRNA(NNU)
CC       t(6)A37 threonylcarbamoyladenosine modification in vitro in
CC       E.coli. {ECO:0000305|PubMed:22378793}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the KAE1 / TsaD family. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-
CC       Rule:MF_01445}.
CC   -!- CAUTION: Was proposed to be a ligase for bicarbonate and threonine
CC       (PubMed:21285948): in the first step, TsaD would catalyze transfer
CC       of a gamma-phosphate group from ATP to bicarbonate, yielding
CC       carboxyphosphate and ADP; carboxyphosphate would then react with
CC       the threonine amine, producing N-carbamoylthreonine and phosphate.
CC       However, the protein ortholog in B.subtilis was shown to be
CC       involved in another step of t(6)A37 biosynthesis, i.e. the
CC       transfer of the threonylcarbamoyl moiety of threonylcarbamoyl-AMP
CC       (TC-AMP) to the N6 group of A37, together with TsaE and TsaB
CC       (PubMed:23072323). The protein ortholog in yeast (QRI7) was also
CC       shown to catalyze this step, and does not require the presence of
CC       additional proteins (PubMed:23620299).
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:21285948}.
CC   -!- CAUTION: The well-known t(6)A modification appears to be a
CC       hydrolyzed artifact of natural cyclic t(6)A (ct(6)A) that occurs
CC       during the preparation and handling of tRNA in E.coli and many
CC       other species (PubMed:23242255). In these species, the t(6)A
CC       modification is processed further by dehydration into ct(6)A, a
CC       reaction catalyzed by TcdA. {ECO:0000305}.
