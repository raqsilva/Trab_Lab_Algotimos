CC   -!- FUNCTION: Metallopeptidase that binds to the auxin transport
CC       inhibitor N-1-naphthylphthalamic acid (NPA). Required for
CC       embryonic and seedling development as well as cell cycle
CC       progression. Homodimerization is required to proper localization
CC       and activity. May play a negative role in the regulation of PIN
CC       auxin transport proteins. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11891249,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19531600, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20154099,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23065197}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Release of an N-terminal amino acid, Xaa-|-
CC       Yaa- from a peptide, amide or arylamide. Xaa is preferably Ala,
CC       but may be most amino acids including Pro (slow action). When a
CC       terminal hydrophobic residue is followed by a prolyl residue, the
CC       two may be released as an intact Xaa-Pro dipeptide.
CC   -!- COFACTOR:

CC         Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:11891249};
CC       Note=Binds 1 zinc ion per subunit. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11891249};

CC         Vmax=59 nmol/min/mg enzyme for Tyr-aminofluoromethylcoumarin

CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. Interacts with N-1-naphthylphthalamic acid

CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Membrane; Peripheral membrane protein.
CC       Microsome membrane; Peripheral membrane protein. Cytoplasm.
CC       Note=The dileucine internalization motif may be involved in
CC       intracellular sequestration.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Ubiquitous with preferential expression in 5
CC       days-old seedlings, roots, young flowers, upper inflorescence
CC       stems, and rosette leaves. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11891249,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19531600}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Expression starts at 3 days and peaks at 5
CC       days. After, expression levels remain constant from 7 to 10 days.
CC       During embryogenesis, expressed first at the site of root meristem
CC       formation, then in the epidernal and ground tissue, root meristem
CC       and suspensor. In the mature embryo, expressed in the vascular
CC       primordia throughout the hypocotyl/root axis.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11891249, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19531600}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Dileucine motif seems to be involved in protein-protein
CC       interactions.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Loss-of-function mutants show irregular,
CC       uncoordinated cell divisions throughout embryogenesis, affecting
CC       the shape and number of cotyledons and the hypophysis, and is
CC       seedling lethal at 5 days after germination due to root growth
CC       arrest. Quiescent center and cell cycle markers show no signals in
CC       apm1-1 knockdown mutants, and the ground tissue specifiers
CC       SHORTROOT and SCARECROW are misexpressed or mislocalized. apm1
CC       mutants have multiple, fused cotyledons and hypocotyls with
CC       enlarged epidermal cells with cell adhesion defects. apm1 alleles
CC       show defects in gravitropism and auxin transport.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19531600}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the peptidase M1 family. {ECO:0000305}.
