CC   -!- FUNCTION: Plays a central role in 2-thiolation of mcm(5)S(2)U at
CC       tRNA wobble positions of cytosolic tRNA(Lys), tRNA(Glu) and
CC       tRNA(Gln). Acts by mediating the C-terminal thiocarboxylation of
CC       sulfur carrier URM1. Its N-terminus first activates URM1 as acyl-
CC       adenylate (-COAMP), then the persulfide sulfur on the catalytic
CC       cysteine is transferred to URM1 to form thiocarboxylation (-COSH)
CC       of its C-terminus. The reaction probably involves hydrogen sulfide
CC       that is generated from the persulfide intermediate and that acts
CC       as nucleophile towards URM1. Subsequently, a transient disulfide
CC       bond is formed. Does not use thiosulfate as sulfur donor; NFS1
CC       probably acting as a sulfur donor for thiocarboxylation reactions.
CC       Prior mcm(5) tRNA modification by the elongator complex is
CC       required for 2-thiolation. May also be involved in protein
CC       urmylation. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_03049}.
