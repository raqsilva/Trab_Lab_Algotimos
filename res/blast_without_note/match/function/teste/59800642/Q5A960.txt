CC   -!- FUNCTION: Responsible for the deacetylation of lysine residues on
CC       the N-terminal part of the core histones (H2A, H2B, H3 and H4).
CC       Histone deacetylation gives a tag for epigenetic repression and
CC       plays an important role in transcriptional regulation, cell cycle
CC       progression and developmental events. Histone deacetylases act via
CC       the formation of large multiprotein complexes. Deacetylates the
CC       YNG2 subunit of NuA4 histone acetyltransferase (HAT) module,
CC       leading to the reduction of YNG2 and NuA4 HAT at the promoters of
CC       hypha-specific genes. Plays a key role in the regulation of
CC       filamentous growth and virulence. Involved in the switch between
CC       two heritable states, the white and opaque states. These two cell
CC       types differ in many characteristics, including cell structure,
CC       mating competence, and virulence. Each state is heritable for many
CC       generations, and switching between states occurs stochastically at
CC       low frequency. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11404352,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11443097, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12798685,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20730094, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21811397,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22536157, ECO:0000269|PubMed:23041319}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Hydrolysis of an N(6)-acetyl-lysine residue of
CC       a histone to yield a deacetylated histone.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with BRG1. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22536157}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22536157}.
CC       Note=Recruited to promoters of hypha-specific genes by BRG1 in a
CC       rapamycin-dependent manner.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the histone deacetylase family. HD type 2
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
