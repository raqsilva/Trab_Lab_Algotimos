CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalytic component of the RPD3 histone deacetylase
CC       (HDAC) complexes RPD3C(L) and RPD3C(S) responsible for the
CC       deacetylation of lysine residues on the N-terminal part of the
CC       core histones (H2A, H2B, H3 and H4). Histone deacetylation plays
CC       an important role in transcriptional regulation, cell cycle
CC       progression, DNA damage response, osmotic stress response and
CC       developmental events. Is involved in rDNA and telomere silencing
CC       and in double strand breaks repair. Required for both full
CC       transcription repression and activation of many genes including
CC       cell type-specific genes (STE6, TY2 and HO), cell differentiation-
CC       specific genes (SPO13), genes that respond to external signals
CC       (PHO5) and TRK2. The RPD3 complexes regulate also chromosomal
CC       replication timing. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10079324,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10359799, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10388812,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10931932, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11069890,

CC       EAF3, RCO1, RPD3, SIN3, and UME1. Interacts with cyclophilins
