CC   -!- FUNCTION: DEAD-box RNA helicase involved in various cellular
CC       processes at low temperature, including ribosome biogenesis, mRNA
CC       degradation and translation initiation. Exhibits RNA-stimulated
CC       ATP hydrolysis and RNA unwinding activity at low temperature.
CC       Involved in 50S ribosomal subunit assembly, acting after SrmB, and
CC       could also play a role in the biogenesis of the 30S ribosomal
CC       subunit. In addition, is involved in mRNA decay, via formation of
CC       a cold-shock degradosome with RNase E. Also stimulates translation
CC       of some mRNAs, probably at the level of initiation.
CC       {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_00964, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10216955,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15148362, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15196029,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15554978, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17259309,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8552679}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + H(2)O = ADP + phosphate.
CC       {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_00964, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15196029,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17259309}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with the 50S ribosomal subunit upon shifting to
CC       15 degrees Celsius. Also found associated with the RNA degradosome
CC       at 15 degrees Celsius; binds RNase E (rne).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15148362, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15554978,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8552679}.

CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8552679}.
