CC   -!- FUNCTION: Member of the two-component regulatory system ArlS/ArlR
CC       involved in the regulation of adhesion, autolysis, multidrug
CC       resistance and virulence. ArlS/ArlR affects expression of the
CC       multidrug resistance transporter NorA and interacts with both Agr
CC       (virulence accessory gene regulator) (negatively) and SarA
CC       (staphylococcal accessory regulator) (positively) to modulate
CC       several virulence factor genes, including ssp (serine protease),
CC       spa (surface protein A) and hla (alpha-hemolysin). Could inhibit
CC       biofilm development by a mechanism independent of the presence of
CC       the poly-N-acetylglucosamine (PNAG). Also, Arl proteins are
CC       required for the efficient activity of DNA gyrase inhibitors and
CC       high osmolarity on spa expression. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10633099,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10869073, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11454217,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15528666, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16030226}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Activated by Agr, SarA, SarV and MgrA.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylated by ArlS. {ECO:0000305}.
