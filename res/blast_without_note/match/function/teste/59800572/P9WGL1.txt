CC   -!- FUNCTION: Member of the two-component regulatory system MprB/MprA
CC       which contributes to maintaining a balance among several systems
CC       involved in stress resistance and is required for establishment
CC       and maintenance of persistent infection in the host. In response
CC       to environmental signals MprB acts as both a membrane-associated
CC       protein kinase that undergoes autophosphorylation and subsequently
CC       transfers the phosphate to MprA, and a protein phosphatase that
CC       dephosphorylates phospho-MprA. MprB/MprA is involved in regulation
CC       of numerous stress-responsive genes, including up-regulation of
CC       two sigma factors, sigE and sigB as well as pepD and mprA, and
CC       repression of multiple genes from regulons associated with
CC       hypoxia, starvation and iron metabolism. The majority of genes
CC       regulated by MprB/MprA under a particular stress condition are
CC       different from those induced during normal growth, but several
CC       genes are commonly regulated under more than one condition.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11675502, ECO:0000269|PubMed:14638785,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15601704, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16513743,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17379732}.
