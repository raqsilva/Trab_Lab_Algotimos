CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes a reaction of the Smirnoff-Wheeler pathway,
CC       the major route to ascorbate biosynthesis in plants. Plays an
CC       essential role in plant growth and development and cell-wall
CC       architecture. Provides GDP-mannose, used for cell wall
CC       carbohydrate biosynthesis, protein N-glycosylation, as well as for
CC       the biosynthesis of the antioxidant ascorbate.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10097187, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10655235,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11226227, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12671089,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15064386, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16244149,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16720601, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18849295,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19011088, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8790441,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9390448}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: GTP + alpha-D-mannose 1-phosphate =
CC       diphosphate + GDP-mannose.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Nucleotide-sugar biosynthesis; GDP-alpha-D-mannose
CC       biosynthesis; GDP-alpha-D-mannose from alpha-D-mannose 1-phosphate
CC       (GTP route): step 1/1.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Embryonic lethal. Deficient in N-
CC       glycosylation, altered in cytokinesis and cell wall architecture
CC       during embryogenesis. Mutants vtc1 and hsn1 with reduced enzyme
CC       activity show reduced root length and leaf cell size, reduced
CC       accumulation of ascorbate, increased sensitivity to ozone, UV-B
CC       and oxidative stresses, increased levels of abscisic acid (ABA),
CC       salicylic acid (SA) and resistance to virulent pathogens and root
CC       growth inhibition in the presence of NH(4)(+).
