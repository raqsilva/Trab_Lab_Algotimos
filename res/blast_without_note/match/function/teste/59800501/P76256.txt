CC   -!- FUNCTION: Required for the formation of a threonylcarbamoyl group
CC       on adenosine at position 37 (t(6)A37) in tRNAs that read codons
CC       beginning with adenine. Is probably involved in the transfer of
CC       the threonylcarbamoyl moiety of threonylcarbamoyl-AMP (TC-AMP) to
CC       the N6 group of A37, together with TsaD and TsaE. TsaB seems to
CC       play an indirect role in the t(6)A biosynthesis pathway, possibly
CC       in regulating the core enzymatic function of TsaD. In fact, can
CC       act as a protease that specifically degrades TsaD in vitro;
CC       therefore TsaB may post-translationally regulate cellular pools of
CC       TsaD via proteolytic degradation. Does not show sialoglycoprotease
CC       activity against glycophorin A. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19376873,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22378793}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. Interacts with TsaD and TsaE in a mutually
CC       exclusive manner; TsaD is the preferred partner. Also interacts
CC       with TsaC. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19376873,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20701780, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22378793}.
CC   -!- INTERACTION:
CC       P05852:tsaD; NbExp=6; IntAct=EBI-560669, EBI-561994;
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19376873}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Appears essential for growth, since no null
CC       mutants can be obtained. Conditional depletion of this gene leads
CC       to enlarged cells, which display highly condensed nucleoids. The
CC       TsaB depletion phenotype is suppressed by overexpressing the
CC       response regulator RstA. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19376873}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: TsaBCDE are necessary and sufficient for tRNA(NNU)
CC       t(6)A37 threonylcarbamoyladenosine modification in vitro in
CC       E.coli. {ECO:0000305|PubMed:22378793}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the KAE1 / TsaD family. TsaB subfamily.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- CAUTION: The well-known t(6)A modification appears to be a
CC       hydrolyzed artifact of natural cyclic t(6)A (ct(6)A) that occurs
CC       during the preparation and handling of tRNA in E.coli and many
CC       other species (PubMed:23242255). In these species, the t(6)A
CC       modification is processed further by dehydration into ct(6)A, a
CC       reaction catalyzed by TcdA. {ECO:0000305}.
