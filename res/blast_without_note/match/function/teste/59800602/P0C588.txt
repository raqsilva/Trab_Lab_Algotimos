CC   -!- FUNCTION: Probable metal transporter. The interaction with the
CC       metal ion chaperone COX11 suggests that it may play a role in
CC       sensory neuron functions. May play a role in biomineralization and
CC       retinal function. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15840172,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19200525}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with COX11. {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000305|PubMed:15840172};
CC       Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000305|PubMed:15840172}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Present in spinal cord dorsal horn neurons and
CC       in developing teeth (at protein level). In the tooth, higher
CC       expression is found in the ameloblasts during the transition and
CC       maturation phases of amelogenesis; reduced eypression in the
CC       odontoblasts. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15840172,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19200525}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Shares weak sequence similarity with the cyclin
CC       family, hence its name. However, it has no cyclin-like function in
CC       vivo.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the ACDP family. {ECO:0000305}.
