CC   -!- FUNCTION: Site-specific tyrosine recombinase, which acts by
CC       catalyzing the cutting and rejoining of the recombining DNA
CC       molecules. Binds cooperatively to specific DNA consensus sequences
CC       that are separated from XerD binding sites by a short central
CC       region, forming the heterotetrameric XerC-XerD complex that
CC       recombines DNA substrates. The complex is essential to convert
CC       dimers of the bacterial chromosome into monomers to permit their
CC       segregation at cell division. It also contributes to the
CC       segregational stability of plasmids at ColE1 xer (or cer) and
CC       pSC101 (or psi) sites. In the complex XerC specifically exchanges
CC       the top DNA strands (By similarity). {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: During recombination, the heterotetrameric
CC       complex catalyzes two consecutive pairs of strand exchanges,
CC       implying that specific pairs of active sites are sequentially
CC       switched on and off in the recombinase tetramer to ensure that
CC       appropriate DNA strands will be exchanged at both reaction steps.
CC       FtsK plays a central role in this catalytic state switch that
CC       turns recombinase on and off reciprocally. The reciprocal C-
CC       terminal interaction between XerC and XerD may also participate in
CC       the enzymatic switch process. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10635320,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11832210}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Forms a cyclic heterotetrameric complex composed of two
CC       molecules of XerC and two molecules of XerD, in which XerC
CC       interacts with XerD via its C-terminal region, XerD interacts with
CC       XerC via its C-terminal region and so on.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10361305}.
