CC   -!- FUNCTION: Part of the twin-arginine translocation (Tat) system
CC       that transports large folded proteins containing a characteristic
CC       twin-arginine motif in their signal peptide across membranes.
CC       Required for PhoD secretion. TatCd promotes membrane localization
CC       of TatAd via domain specific interactions. TatCd is required for
CC       stabile production of TatAd as well as for its maintenance.
CC       {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_00902, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18029357}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Forms a complex with TatAd. Two types of complexes exist:
CC       one composed of TatAd and TatCd, and another composed only of
CC       TatAd. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_00902,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18029357}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-
CC       Rule:MF_00902, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12867413}; Multi-pass membrane
CC       protein {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_00902,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12867413}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Expressed under conditions of phosphate starvation.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Depletion of TatCd results in a drastic
CC       reduction of TatAd. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16698798}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: B.subtilis possesses two minimal, substrate-
CC       specific, Tat translocases: TatAd-TatCd and TatAy-TatCy, each one
CC       composed of a TatA and a TatC protein. TatA is bifunctional and
CC       performs the function of both the TatA and TatB proteins of Gram-
CC       negative organisms.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the TatC family. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-
CC       Rule:MF_00902}.
