CC   -!- FUNCTION: Part of the twin-arginine translocation (Tat) system
CC       that transports large folded proteins containing a characteristic
CC       twin-arginine motif in their signal peptide across the thylakoid
CC       membrane. Involved in delta pH-dependent protein transport
CC       required for chloroplast development, especially thylakoid
CC       membrane formation. TATC and TATB mediate precursor recognition,
CC       whereas TATA facilitates translocation.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11526245}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: In thylakoid membranes, TATC and TATB form a large
CC       receptor complex, containing about eight TATC-TATB pairs, which
CC       binds the precursor protein. Twin arginine signal peptide promotes
CC       pH-triggered docking of TATA oligomers to TATC-TATB receptor
CC       complex, inducing a conformational switch of TATA that results in
CC       activation of the translocase. TATA dissociates from TATC-TATB
CC       upon completion of translocation. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18930082}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Plastid, chloroplast thylakoid membrane
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:11526245}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:11526245}. Note=The N-terminus is located in
CC       the stroma. {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Seedling lethality when homozygous. When
CC       grown on agar medium, mutant seedlings have an albino phenotype
CC       and contain plastid lacking internal membrane structures.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11526245}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: According to PubMed:18930082, TATA is detectable
CC       only in minor amounts in Arabidopsis chloroplasts.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the TatC family. {ECO:0000305}.
