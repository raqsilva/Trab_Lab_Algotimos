CC   -!- FUNCTION: Part of the twin-arginine translocation (Tat) system
CC       that transports large folded proteins containing a characteristic
CC       twin-arginine motif in their signal peptide across the thylakoid
CC       membrane. Involved in delta pH-dependent protein transport
CC       required for chloroplast development, especially thylakoid
CC       membrane formation. TATC and TATB mediate precursor recognition,
CC       whereas TATA facilitates translocation.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11457457, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11502764,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19207210, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22564412}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: In thylakoid membranes, TATC and TATB form a large
CC       receptor complex, containing about eight TATC-TATB pairs, which
CC       binds the precursor protein. Twin arginine signal peptide promotes
CC       pH-triggered docking of TATA oligomers to TATC-TATB receptor
CC       complex, inducing a conformational switch of TATA that results in
CC       activation of the translocase. TATA dissociates from TATC-TATB
CC       upon completion of translocation. According to PubMed:22564412, it
CC       is estimated that the translocase fully saturated with precursor
CC       proteins and TATA is an 2.2-megadalton complex that can
CC       individually transport eight precursor proteins or cooperatively
CC       transport multimeric precursors. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11502764,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11956224, ECO:0000269|PubMed:14653819,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19207210, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22564412}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Plastid, chloroplast thylakoid membrane
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:11457457, ECO:0000305|PubMed:14653819,
CC       ECO:0000305|PubMed:19207210, ECO:0000305|PubMed:22564412}; Multi-
CC       pass membrane protein {ECO:0000305|PubMed:11457457,
CC       ECO:0000305|PubMed:14653819, ECO:0000305|PubMed:19207210,
CC       ECO:0000305|PubMed:22564412}. Note=The N-terminus is located in
CC       the stroma.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: TATC targets to thylakoids via a stromal
CC       intermediate, and then probably integrated through a thylakoid
CC       translocation pathway. {ECO:0000305|PubMed:22564412}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the TatC family. {ECO:0000305}.
